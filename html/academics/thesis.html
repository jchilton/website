John's PhD Thesis

Byrneglish

Byrneglish is a dialect of English in which all communication is suggestive of the future of the person or people that the Byrneglish speaker is talking with or about. In this regard Byrneglish is similar to Foucault's "subjectification." The primary difference between Byrneglish and subjectification is that Byrneglish is conversational, that is, all speaking parties participate in the development of each other, whereas subjectification is an inquisitive activity in which an authority on personality, personal depth, personal "growth" or "being-in-the-world" "rescues" other(s) from "inside-outness," or, lack of uniqueness. Said more simply, Byrneglish is a fair and equal trade in learning about while developing others' true selves, whereas subjectification is a one-sided effort by a party to develop another party, at some cost to the former.

Above is the original thesis, which deserves much treatment. A primary note that must be made and should have been included in the thesis is the comparison and contrast of Byrneglish and objectification, which is development of another in a way that that other may or may not want to be developed, and usually occurs at a cost to the objectified and benefit to the objectifier — examples of objectification include visits with a medical doctor or talking to the police.
